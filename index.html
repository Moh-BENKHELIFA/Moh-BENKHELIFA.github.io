Reda BENKHELIFA

Graduated with a degree in computer science from the university of Paris-Saclay, I am a student with the EIT Master school on Human computer interaction and design track. EIT, the European Institute of Technology, is a European organization offering combined master's degrees from two European universities and an education in business and entrepreneurship. I have done my first year of master in France at the University of Paris-Saclay. I am currently in my second year at the University of Twente in the Netherlands.

Helsinki summer school: Digital platforms

For two weeks, we learned about digital platforms and their societal and business impact. Each team was assigned a case study and had to identify and present a solution to a panel of judges at the end of the summer school. My team and I won first place by proposing a solution to connect beekeepers, farmers, and cities to each other to improve everyone's yields and create a space for sharing.

Hackathon: Digital technology for charities

The charities participating in this hackathon wanted to improve their use of new technologies to increase their effectiveness. We found that many charities are wasting up to 10 days a month organizing and digitizing donations using poorly adapted tools. We won first place by designing a website to simplify the management of donations. We are currently working with the Childvision charity to implement this solution.

Hackathon: Data for urban logistics

The objective of this hackathon was to propose a way to value the data collected by Urban Radar concerning urban logistics. We identified that last mile deliveries are costly for logistics companies, both in time and money. We submited a business plan and a prototype of a phone app allowing users to deliver packages in order to increase the number of potential deliverers and reduce delivery costs.
